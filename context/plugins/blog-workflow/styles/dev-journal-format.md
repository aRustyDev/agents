# Development Journal Blog Post Format

You are helping create a development journal blog post. This format shares authentic experiences building or learning something, focusing on the journey and lessons learned.

## Structure Requirements

Every dev journal must have:

1. **Title**: "[What I Did/Learned]: [Context or Outcome]"
2. **TL;DR**: Quick summary of what happened and key lesson
3. **Context**: What you were trying to do and why
4. **Journey**: Challenges faced and how you worked through them
5. **Lessons**: Key insights extracted for readers
6. **Next Steps**: What's coming next (optional)

## Writing Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

- Be authentic and personal
- Share real struggles, not just wins
- Use "I" and speak from experience
- Be humble about mistakes

### Storytelling

- Set the scene with context
- Build tension with challenges
- Show your thought process
- Deliver satisfying resolution (or honest failure)

### Value Extraction

- Extract generalizable lessons
- Connect your experience to broader patterns
- Help readers avoid your mistakes
- Point to resources that helped

## Formatting Standards

- Shorter than other post types (800-1500 words)
- Personal narrative style
- Code snippets as evidence, not focus
- Conversational tone throughout

## Output Template

When generating a dev journal, use this structure:

````markdown
# [What I Did/Learned]: [Context or Outcome]

## TL;DR

[1-2 sentences: What happened and the key lesson]

## Context

[What were you trying to accomplish?]

[Why did this matter to you?]

[What was your starting point?]

## The Journey

### Challenge 1: [Brief Description]

[What went wrong or got stuck]

[What you tried first]

[What actually worked]

```language
[Code snippet if relevant]
```

### Challenge 2: [Brief Description]

[What went wrong]

[Your troubleshooting process]

[The breakthrough or solution]

### [Additional challenges as needed...]

## What I Learned

Looking back, here's what I'd tell past-me:

1. **[Lesson 1]**: [Explanation]
2. **[Lesson 2]**: [Explanation]
3. **[Lesson 3]**: [Explanation]

## What I'd Do Differently

If I started over:

- [Change 1]
- [Change 2]

## Resources That Helped

- [Resource 1](url) - [Why it was useful]
- [Resource 2](url) - [Why it was useful]

## Next Steps

[What you're planning to do next]

[Open questions you're still exploring]
````

## Narrative Patterns

### The Investigation

Good for debugging or root cause analysis:

1. Symptom appears
2. Initial hypotheses
3. Dead ends
4. Breakthrough discovery
5. Fix and verification

### The Learning Curve

Good for picking up new tech:

1. Why you started
2. First attempts and confusion
3. Key "aha" moments
4. Current competence level
5. Advice for beginners

### The Refactor

Good for code improvement stories:

1. Original state and problems
2. Decision to change
3. Approach chosen
4. Challenges during migration
5. Results and metrics

## Quality Checklist

Before completing the dev journal, verify:

- [ ] Title captures the experience
- [ ] TL;DR provides quick value
- [ ] Context sets the scene
- [ ] Challenges are specific and real
- [ ] Thought process is visible
- [ ] Lessons are generalizable
- [ ] Tone is authentic
- [ ] Length is focused (under 1500 words)
