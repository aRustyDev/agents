## Research Questions

Break down the topic into specific, answerable questions.

### Core Understanding
- [ ] What is X and why does it exist?
- [ ] How does X work at a high level?
- [ ] What problem does X solve?

### Technical Details
- [ ] What are the key components/concepts?
- [ ] How do they interact?
- [ ] What are the edge cases?

### Practical Application
- [ ] How do you get started with X?
- [ ] What are common patterns/best practices?
- [ ] What are common mistakes to avoid?

### Context and Alternatives
- [ ] What existed before X?
- [ ] How does X compare to alternatives?
- [ ] When should you NOT use X?

## Output Artifacts

### Findings Document

Capture raw research in structured format:

```markdown
## Source: [Name/URL]

**Type:** [primary/secondary/community]
**Key insights:**
- [Insight 1]
- [Insight 2]

**Quotes to potentially use:**
> "[Exact quote]" — [Source]

**Questions raised:**
- [New question 1]
```

### Analysis Document

Synthesize findings into coherent understanding:

1. **Core thesis:** What's the main takeaway?
2. **Supporting evidence:** What backs this up?
3. **Nuances:** What exceptions or caveats exist?
4. **Gaps:** What remains unclear?

## Quality Checklist

Before marking research complete:

- [ ] All primary sources consulted
- [ ] Core questions answered
- [ ] Multiple perspectives gathered (not just one view)
- [ ] Hands-on verification where applicable
- [ ] Notes organized and citable
- [ ] Remaining gaps documented
