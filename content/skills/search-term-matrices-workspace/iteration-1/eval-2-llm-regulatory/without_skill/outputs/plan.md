# Research Plan: LLM Regulatory Landscape

## Objective

Map the regulatory requirements for large language model providers across the EU, US, and China, and identify enforcement precedents.

## Phase 1: EU AI Act

Research the EU AI Act's foundation model provisions.

**Search terms:**
- "EU AI Act foundation model requirements"
- "EU AI Act general purpose AI obligations"
- "AI Act risk classification system"

**Key questions:**
- What are the transparency obligations for foundation model providers?
- How are general-purpose AI systems classified under the risk framework?
- What are the penalties for non-compliance?

## Phase 2: US Regulatory Framework

Research the NIST AI RMF and executive orders.

**Search terms:**
- "NIST AI Risk Management Framework"
- "Executive Order AI safety 2023"
- "US AI regulation compliance requirements"

**Key questions:**
- What voluntary frameworks exist for LLM providers?
- How does NIST AI RMF apply to generative AI specifically?
- Are there sector-specific requirements (healthcare, finance)?

## Phase 3: China Regulations

Research China's Interim Measures for generative AI.

**Search terms:**
- "China generative AI regulations 2023"
- "China interim measures generative AI"
- "CAC algorithm registration requirements"

**Key questions:**
- What are the content filtering requirements?
- What is the algorithm registration process?
- How do the regulations apply to foreign companies?

## Phase 4: Enforcement and Case Studies

Find documented enforcement actions or regulatory audits.

**Search terms:**
- "AI regulation enforcement action"
- "LLM regulatory audit"
- "AI compliance penalty fine"
- "GDPR AI decision making enforcement"

## Phase 5: Comparative Analysis

Create a jurisdiction comparison covering:
- Scope of regulation (all AI vs. generative AI only)
- Provider obligations
- Risk classification approach
- Enforcement mechanism
- Penalty structure
- Extraterritorial reach

## Timeline

- Phase 1-3: Jurisdiction research (2 hours)
- Phase 4: Enforcement research (1 hour)
- Phase 5: Comparative synthesis (1 hour)

## Risks

- Regulations are evolving rapidly; information may be outdated within months
- Chinese regulations may lack authoritative English translations
- Enforcement actions may not be publicly documented in all jurisdictions
